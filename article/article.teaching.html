Cryptography and Its Applications (core course for Information Security major)

Textbook: Understanding Cryptography: A Textbook for Students and Practitioners by Christof Paar and Jan Pelzl

This course offers a landscape overview of cryptography from ancient word tricks to modern day algorithms. Instead of putting weights on discrete math inductions, this course focuses on the rationale behind the evolution of cryptographic techniques and how these techniques support historical and modern applications. Every year, this course updates itself to keep up with the frontier of cryptography research and adds fresh ingredients into the syllabus. Modules such as POODLE attacks and SM series ciphers are already included in the past year teaching.


Computer Networks (core course for computer science and related majors)

Textbook: Computer Networking: A Top-Down Approach by James Kurose and Keith Ross

This course focuses on the general understanding of modern Internet as the network of networks. The teaching follows the top-down approach to deliver network concepts layer by layer. From the daily applications such as web surfing and instant messaging to the physical foundation of Internet such as the trans-ocean cables and satellites, this course explores dozens of typical Internet components and techniques to help students form a comprehensive understanding to the Internet.
